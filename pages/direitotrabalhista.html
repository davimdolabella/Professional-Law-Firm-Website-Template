What is Labor Law?

Labor Law is the legal field that governs the relationship between employers and employees, establishing rights and duties for both parties. Its main objective is to ensure dignified working conditions, protect workers, and balance labor relations, considering the vulnerability of employees in relation to the economic power of employers.

Smith Legal Services offers specialized legal advice on labor issues, serving both workers and companies, with strategic solutions to resolve conflicts and prevent litigation in employment relationships.

Key Legislation

Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)

Establishes minimum wage, overtime pay, recordkeeping, and youth employment standards affecting employees in the private sector and in federal, state, and local governments, setting the foundation for employment relationships.

National Labor Relations Act (NLRA)

Guarantees the right of employees to organize and bargain collectively with their employers, and to engage in other protected concerted activity with or without a union, or to refrain from all such activity.

Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)

Provides eligible employees with up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per year for specified family and medical reasons, with continuation of group health insurance coverage.

Occupational Safety and Health Act (OSHA)

Ensures safe and healthful working conditions for workers by setting and enforcing standards and by providing training, outreach, education and assistance to employers and employees.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the statute of limitations for filing a labor complaint?

+

For most FLSA (Fair Labor Standards Act) claims, such as unpaid wages or overtime, you have 2 years from the date of the violation to file a lawsuit, or 3 years if the violation was willful. For discrimination claims under Title VII, you must first file with the EEOC within 180 days (or 300 days in states with their own anti-discrimination laws).

What elements are required to establish an employer-employee relationship?

+

To establish an employer-employee relationship, courts typically apply the "economic realities test" which examines: the employer's control over the worker, the worker's opportunity for profit or loss, the worker's investment in equipment, the skill required, the permanence of the relationship, and whether the service is integral to the employer's business. The Department of Labor also uses variations of this test to determine employee status versus independent contractor classification.

How does a labor settlement agreement work?

+

A labor settlement agreement is a legally binding contract that resolves disputes between employers and employees. The process can occur through the Department of Labor, the EEOC, private mediation, or court. The agreement should be in writing with clearly specified terms and typically includes a release of claims in exchange for compensation. Most settlements must be approved by the relevant agency or court to be fully enforceable and may include confidentiality provisions.

What documents should I bring to a labor law consultation?

+

For an effective labor law consultation, you should bring: employment contract, offer letter, employee handbook, pay stubs, W-2 forms, time records, performance evaluations, disciplinary notices, termination letter (if applicable), any written communications with your employer (emails, texts), benefits information, and any documentation related to the specific issue you're consulting about (discrimination, harassment, retaliation, etc.).
